Hi, I'm Raisa!

You'll find me somewhere, always asking questions.


And I'm never afraid to speak my mind.

I was created and packaged in Jakarta, Indonesia for the first 18 years of my life.

Then, I was exported to Davis, California and repackaged even further. I exposed myself to a breadth of subjects, ranging from STEM to social sciences and the humanities: I learned programming, economics, anthropology, psychology, music, English composition, and the list goes on. I majored in Computer Science and minored in Music at the University of California, Davis, and I completed my college education in a little over three years.

Next, I was shipped to Massachusetts, where I worked at Bose's Alexa Voice Cloud Team. There, I stretched my abilities as an engineer, and I was an apprentice to the most amazing and accomplished mentors who have now become my friends.

After that, I continued my adventure as a full-stack software engineer at Trimble MAPS. There, I learned how to learn: I was thrown in the water, and I learned how to swim. From a software development standpoint, I learned everything from frontend to backend development to software infrastructure. From a personal development standpoint, I gained confidence. I learned to question everything, and I was encouraged to ask uncomfortable questions without shame or retribution. I discovered what it means to be a leader, and it's not about having a certain title or an assigned position of seniority. Leadership is all about taking initiative, inspiring people, and bringing out the best in others.

I started and currently host Intrigued The Podcast, where I talk to people from different industries. This podcast is for anyone, especially young people, who may be feeling lost with regards to their career path. Through conversations with our guests, we expose a wide array of life paths and career options in order to show young people that there is no one path to a fulfilling career.

I may be repackaged or shipped to other stepping stones in life, but you will find me doing the same two things: asking questions and building relationships along the way.
